cotoletta

What is a cotoletta alla milanese

The "cotoletta alla milanese" is a traditional Italian dish from Milan. It consists of a breaded and fried veal cutlet, typically cooked with the bone in. It's similar to the Austrian Wiener Schnitzel but is usually thicker and cooked in butter.

  • Veal cutlet (bone-in, traditionally from the rib)
  • Eggs
  • Breadcrumbs/li>
  • Butter (for frying)
  • Salt

How to make it

  1. Take a bone-in veal cutlet and gently flatten it if necessary.
  2. Lightly season the meat with salt.
  3. Beat the eggs in a bowl.
  4. Dip the cutlet into the beaten eggs, making sure it's fully coated.
  5. Press the cutlet into breadcrumbs, coating it evenly on both sides. Do not use flour.
  6. In a pan, melt butter over medium heat.
  7. Fry the cutlet in the butter until golden brown on both sides (about 5–6 minutes per side
  8. Remove from the pan and drain on paper towels to remove excess butter.
  9. Serve hot, traditionally with a wedge of lemon or a side of salad or potatoes.
