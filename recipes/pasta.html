Pasta carbonara

what is the carbonara souce?

Pasta carbonara is a traditional Italian dish made with eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, cured pork (usually guanciale), and black pepper. It's typically served with spaghetti or another long pasta, and does not contain cream. The heat from the pasta cooks the egg mixture, creating a creamy, flavorful sauce.

main engridient:

  • spaghetti
  • guangiale
  • eggs
  • pecorino romano cheese
  • black pepper

How to make it

  1. Boil water and cook spaghetti (about 100g per person) until al dente.
  2. Pasta cooks, cut guanciale (or pancetta) into small pieces and cook in a pan until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, mix 2 egg yolks (or 1 egg + 1 yolk) with a handful of grated Pecorino Romano cheese and black pepper.
  4. When pasta is ready, save a cup of the cooking water, then drain it.
  5. Add hot pasta to the bowl with the egg mixture. Stir quickly to create a creamy sauce (add a bit of pasta water if needed).
  6. Mix in the crispy guanciale.
  7. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper on top.
